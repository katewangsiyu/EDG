\textbf{Key observations:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Energy-Force decoupling:} Energy predictions show much higher sensitivity to hyperparameters (CV range: 6.36\%-48.14\%) compared to force predictions (CV range: 0.50\%-1.58\%). This suggests that the ED-aware teacher primarily provides global energy-level information rather than local force-level gradients.
    \item \textbf{Malonaldehyde anomaly:} Malonaldehyde exhibits the highest energy sensitivity (CV = 48.14\%), consistent with its rapid intramolecular proton transfer dynamics that challenge static-conformation-based ED predictions.
    \item \textbf{Symmetry effect:} Benzene, with its high molecular symmetry, shows the lowest energy sensitivity (CV = 6.36\%), indicating that ED predictions for symmetric molecules are more reliable and less dependent on the filtering threshold.
    \item \textbf{Rank discordance:} The sensitivity rankings for energy and force are uncorrelated (e.g., malonaldehyde ranks 1st for energy CV but 7th for force CV), further supporting the energy-force decoupling hypothesis.
\end{itemize}
