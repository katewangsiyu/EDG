\begin{abstract}
% [Polish] Original: Electron density (ED) encodes electronic structure information crucial for molecular property prediction, yet existing geometry-based machine learning force fields largely ignore it.
Electron density (ED) encodes electronic structure information central to molecular property prediction, yet geometry-based machine learning force fields do not explicitly use it. We propose EDG, a cross-modal distillation framework that transfers ED knowledge---represented as multi-view RGB-D images---from an ED-aware teacher to geometry students as privileged information, requiring no ED computation at inference. However, naive distillation assumes uniform teacher reliability, risking negative transfer from erroneous cross-modal predictions. We extend EDG to EDG++ by introducing a pre-trained reliability estimator and an adaptive global-local thresholding mechanism for selective distillation. We provide theoretical analysis (Proposition~1) establishing a sufficient condition under which selective distillation reduces an upper bound on gradient noise---a condition characteristic of heavy-tailed teacher error distributions in cross-modal settings---and show that the bound is conservative under the positive dependence expected in practice. Experiments on QM9 (12 properties $\times$ 3 architectures) and rMD17 (10 molecules) show that EDG++ improves 34/36 QM9 task-model combinations (average 3.2--9.0\%), reduces tail prediction errors by 27--38\% on the hardest 1\% of test samples, and improves energy/force predictions on 17/20 rMD17 tasks.
\end{abstract}
