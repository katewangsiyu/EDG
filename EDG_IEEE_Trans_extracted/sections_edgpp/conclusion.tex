\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:Conclusion}

% [Polish] Original: We have introduced EDG and EDG++, a framework that leverages electron density...followed by electronic structure properties.
We have presented EDG and EDG++, a framework that uses electron density as privileged information to improve geometry-based molecular models through cross-modal distillation. By representing ED as multi-view RGB-D images and training an ED-aware teacher, the approach eliminates quantum chemical calculations at inference. EDG++ adds reliability-aware selective distillation---motivated by a gradient noise analysis (Proposition~\ref{prop:gradient_noise}) identifying sufficient conditions for noise reduction---to avoid negative transfer from unreliable teacher predictions. Experiments show improvements on 34/36 QM9 task-model combinations and 17/20 rMD17 energy/force predictions, with the improvement hierarchy aligning with the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem: thermodynamic properties benefit most, followed by electronic structure properties.

\textbf{Limitations.} (1)~All results are reported with a single random seed (seed=42). While the sign test ($p < 10^{-8}$ for 34/36 QM9 wins) and bootstrap CI analysis (Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_bootstrap}) provide evidence that the improvements are consistent, multi-seed evaluation would strengthen the conclusions. (2)~EDG++ results are reported per-task-best, which provides an optimistic upper bound; the default-threshold configuration captures the majority of gains (Table~\ref{tab:incremental}). (3)~EDG++ on rMD17 is evaluated on SphereNet only; cross-architecture generality on rMD17 remains to be established. (4)~A non-ED teacher control is not included; while indirect evidence supports that gains originate from the ED signal (see Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_contribution}), a formal ablation would be valuable. (5)~The reliability estimator is frozen during downstream tasks; joint fine-tuning may yield further improvements.

% [Polish] Original: Promising extensions include soft weighting schemes...applicable as general tools for robust cross-modal knowledge transfer beyond the molecular learning setting.
\textbf{Future Directions.} Several directions merit investigation: soft weighting schemes that modulate distillation strength continuously, molecule-adaptive distillation weights for conformationally challenging systems, and extension to other privileged information sources such as molecular orbitals or experimental spectra. The scale-invariant threshold $\tau = \mu + \kappa\sigma$ and the reliability-aware filtering mechanism can serve as general components for cross-modal knowledge transfer beyond molecular learning.
